About Me

My path into software didn't start with a traditional CS degree — it started with an MCA and a lot of curiosity about what makes systems fast. Over four years I went from intern to senior engineer, and what's kept me there is the same thing: a preference for problems where the constraint is real — latency, memory, throughput — rather than abstract.

I care disproportionately about local security, developer tooling, and the kind of infrastructure decisions that quietly compound over time. I build for correctness and performance first, and treat complexity as a cost rather than a feature.

Outside of engineering, I tend toward things that move fast and require full attention.
